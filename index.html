How to use this tool

User Manual — works in any modern browser, no installation needed

Follow these steps in order for the clearest understanding of Kanaleneiland's health situation and what can be done about it.

1
Open the Relationship Bubble Map (01). This is your starting point. You will see 15 health factors displayed as coloured bubbles — each colour represents a WHO 6P pillar. Click any bubble to reveal its CBS score, the gap compared to Utrecht's average, the WHO source behind it, and which other factors it connects to. Use the pillar toggle buttons at the top to filter by category. You can also drag bubbles to rearrange the layout.
2
Open the WHO 6P Criteria (02) to understand the comparison. This page shows every official WHO Phase VII benchmark side by side with Kanaleneiland's real measured data. Use the six pillar tabs (People, Place, Participation, Prosperity, Peace, Planet) to navigate. Each criterion shows a clear status badge — meets, fails, or borderline — so you can immediately see where the district stands against international standards. The overall pass rate is 20%.
3
Open the Cascade Map (03) to see how factors connect. Trace exactly how an intervention on one factor flows through to health outcomes. Click any bubble on the left to light up its specific pathways. Line thickness shows how strong the evidence is for each connection — thicker lines mean stronger, more direct evidence.
4
Open the Intervention Sequence (04) for a step-by-step action plan. Click each numbered step to expand the concrete actions recommended for Kanaleneiland, the cascade effects they trigger, and the indicator they target. Steps are ordered by priority: governance first, then economic equity, green space, loneliness, housing, and active travel.
5
Open the Full Intervention Simulator (05) to test scenarios. Use the 20 sliders to adjust each indicator and watch the scores update live. Start with one of the five preset scenarios — try Greenery only first to see how limited a single-pillar approach is, then switch to Full WHO HiAP to see what coordinated action across all six pillars achieves.
6
Open Information Sources (06) for the full evidence base. Every data point and connection in this tool is backed by a listed source. Use this page to look up any specific claim, check the full citation, or understand why a particular causal link was drawn.
